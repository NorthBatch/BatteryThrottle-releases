# Why I made this app

As a professor of battery chemistry (atomic structure and dynamics of cathodes, anodes, and electrolytes), it annoys me when I see my MacBook's OS choosing to do things that are bad for its battery. As Apple so often does, it tries to make the battery controls a little too automatic and that's not ideal if you're a savvy user. If you want to try taking control back, give this app a free test for a couple weeks and see if you prefer it.

I get annoyed whenever I see my laptop unnecessarily *aging its own battery* by charging at **TOO FAST OF A RATE** and then sitting at **TOO HIGH OF A CHARGE** when I'm only going to be using it off the plug, not going anywhere.

So I made a simple app to stop those two behaviors as well as giving me a quick way to boost up to a still-safe preset "travel" level when I know I'll be on the go and needing the battery later on. And I don't want to have to spend time fooling around with anything complicated, so I made everything controlled by just a single click.

The app switches between these three modes:

1. Charge Limiting: charge the battery at a gentle rate and then stop at an optimum charge level when you know you'll be plugged in for a while.

2. Top Up for Travel: charge the pack to a higher (but still safe) preset level at a faster rate before you head out.
   
3. Discharge: ignore the A/C plug and run your hardware off the battery to sink it to a safer level.

# Usage
The main interface is designed to be user friendly:
* The current battery level is shown as a vertical bar on the fuel guage bar.
* Turn charge limiting on or off with the toggle button. Your system will charge in gentle mode (turtle icon in the menu bar) if below the limit.
* Set the target charge level by dragging the padlock left or right.
* If you plan to use your laptop on the go later on, click the top-up button once to rise to the higher travel target (set the limit in the configuration panel).
* If you know you won't travel for a very long time, click the discharge button once to discharge. Use this mode sparingly, sitting at a slightly higher power level is less detrimental than adding mileage to your pack.
* You can set licensing, travel charge level, gentle-charge rate, automatic updates, and permissions on the configuration panel (click the gear icon).
