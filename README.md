BatteryThrottle for MacOS laptops. Free download with free trial period. License for long term use available within the app or on Gumroad.

# Why I made this app

I’m a retired professor of battery chemistry (atomic structure and dynamics of cathodes, anodes, and electrolytes), so it annoys me when I see my MacBook's OS choosing to do things that are bad for its battery. As Apple so often does, it tries to make the battery controls a little too automatic and that's not ideal if you're a savvy user. If you want to try taking control back, give this app a free test for a couple weeks and see if you prefer it.

I get annoyed whenever I'm plugged in and forced to sit there watching my battery sit at a high charge that is aging it unnecessarily. I also get annoyed when I see the my MacBook inducing damage in its own battery by charging up at maximum rate despite me having no need of that. I wanted a simple one-click interface that switches between three modes:

1. Charge Limiting: charge the battery at a safer rate and then stop at an optimum charge level when you know you'll be plugged in for a while.

2. Top Up for Travel: charge the pack to a higher (but still safe) level at a faster rate before you head out.
   
3. Discharge: ignore the A/C plug and run your hardware off the battery to sink it to a safer level. 

I did collaborations with a variety of scientists in the inudstrial Li-ion battery sector when I was an academic, so I have a pretty good guess on the internal chemistry of MacBook cells. I suggest optimum charge levels and rates based on that estimate, given as ranges that encompass the possible stoichiometries, dopants and additives that they might employ. I certainly wouldn't say that MacBooks use a bad battery management system, but that software can only guess about your plans for the day so there's no doubt you'll do better if you take direct control.

# Usage
The main interface is designed to be user friendly:
* The current battery level is shown as a vertical bar on the fuel guage bar.
* Turn charge limiting on or off with the toggle button. Your system will charge in gentle mode (turtle icon in the menu bar) if below the limit.
* Set the target charge level by dragging the padlock left or right.
* If you plan to use your laptop on the go later on, click the top-up button once to rise to the higher travel target (set the limit in the configuration panel).
* If you know you won't travel for a very long time, click the discharge button once to discharge. Use this mode sparingly, sitting at a slightly higher power level is less detrimental than adding mileage to your pack.
* You can set licensing, travel charge level, gentle-charge rate, automatic updates, and permissions on the configuration panel (click the gear icon).
